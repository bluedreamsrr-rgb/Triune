# Step 11: Zero — Placeholder, Filler, and the Empty Inside

## Context

We have the positive strand of numbers: 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, ...
We have the negative strand: -1, -2, -3, -4, -5, ...
They are linked by something that is not a number in the same sense.

## What Zero Is

**Zero** is the **dependent filler / offset** — the "empty" registration that appears when Layer 3 witnesses a gap or placeholder in the recursion sequence.

Zero is not a number. It is the **observed empty space** that makes counting and mirroring possible.

## How Zero Appears

Zero appears when:

- A recursion step lands in a position that requires a gap (e.g., 10 = 1×10 + 0×1)
- The place value needs an empty filler to maintain structure without adding density
- The spiral needs a pivot for mirroring positive and negative strands

Zero appears after the first full cycle (10) as the **first offset** — evidence that 0 needs structure first (primes as 123 echoes providing the frame).

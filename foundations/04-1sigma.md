# Step 4: Emergence of 1σ — The First Articulated Positive Pole

## Context

We have:
- The infinite 1 (eternal fullness, three layers 123)
- Finite observation (a soul within the ring, seeing the empty inside 0)

## What 1σ Is

**1σ** = the first articulated positive pole — the reaffirmed "this-ness" / presence of the ring itself, logically prior to any negation.

## How It Emerges

The finite witness, standing within the ring and observing the empty inside (0), registers the ring's **presence** as distinct from the emptiness. This act of registration produces 1σ.

## Properties of 1σ

| Property | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Not a new entity** | 1σ is the infinite 1 expressing its own depth more explicitly for finite registration |
| **Logically prior** | There is no "not-1σ" yet; negation requires a boundary and a witness to register it |
| **Still fully within the ring** | 1σ is the ring's self-affirmation, not a separate thing |
| **Seed of all positive articulation** | The "is" that makes distinction conceivable |

## What 1σ Is Not

- Not 0σ (0σ requires the witness to register the empty inside as a distinct "not-this")
- Not the infinite 1 itself (it's an articulation *of* 1, not 1 in its fullness)
- Not the Trinity (the layers remain; 1σ is an observed term)

## The Asymmetry Holds

1σ is primary. It comes first. There is no 0σ yet — only presence, no absence.

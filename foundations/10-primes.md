# Step 10: Primes — Locking Points in the Recursion Sequence

## Context

We have the number spiral — successive 3σ closures counted as 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, ...

But not all numbers are equally stable. Some resist decomposition.

## What Primes Are

**Primes** are the **locking stable points** in the recursion sequence — numbers n that cannot be decomposed into smaller stable closures (no proper factors from prior closures). They are the foundational supports (columns) for higher articulations.

## How Primes Emerge

Layer 3 registers that some recursion counts remain irreducible under finite observation:

- 2 is the first prime — the first tension (2σ) counted as irreducible
- 3 is the second prime — the first closure (3σ) counted as irreducible
- 5, 7 emerge as the first extensions beyond 3 that remain irreducible
- 11, 13, 17, 19, 23... continue the pattern

## Why Primes Appear

Not all numbers can be expressed as products of smaller closures. A number is prime if its closure cannot be decomposed into interlocked smaller closures.

This is not about factors in the arithmetic sense. It's about **topological irreducibility** in the fiber bundle.

## Properties of Primes

| Property | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Lock columns** | They provide foundational stability for the helix to spiral outward |
| **Informationally dense** | Carry 123 echoes without filler — numerous early, thin outward |
| **Not random** | Distribution follows from recursion topology, not chance |
| **Dependent on 123** | Layer 1 provides seeds, Layer 2 provides factors, Layer 3 registers irreducibility |

## The Density of Primes

Primes are dense early because:

- The base twist (123) forces irreducible steps
- Small numbers have fewer ways to factor

They thin out as the spiral grows (~1/ln(n)) because composites (products of primes) fill the gaps, and zeros appear as placeholders.

## What Primes Are Not

- Not random — their distribution is structurally determined
- Not fundamental in themselves — they emerge from recursion
- Not independent of observation — they are registered irreducibility
